Workshop

On the Interplay between Register and Socio-Geographic Variation

CRC 1412 - Collaborative Research Centre 1412 "Register"

Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin | June 8–10, 2026

Call for Papers Speakers

Call for Papers

Overview

Organized by Project A09 of the SFB 1412 "Register", this workshop examines the pluridimensional nature of linguistic variation in Spanish. We focus on the complex interplay between diaphasic (register-based), diatopic (geographic), and diastratic (social) dimensions.

The workshop invites research on how situational settings and communicative purposes interact with geographic and social backgrounds to shape linguistic choices. Key areas include the dynamics of diaglossia, the emergence of regional standards, and how register knowledge mediates the selection of variants across the Hispanic world.

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions addressing the interplay between register, social backgrounds, and geographic variation in Spanish and its varieties:

  • Morphosyntactic Variation: Socio-stylistic stratification and register-sensitivity of morphosyntactic variables in Spanish varieties.
  • The Diaglossic Continuum: Interaction between regional standards, regiolects, and national norms from a pluricentric perspective.
  • Register & Situational Parameters: The impact of social distance, communicative intent, and medium on linguistic architecture.
  • Language Ideologies & Variation: Social meaning, prestige, and metalinguistic awareness in the selection of register-specific variants.
  • Standardization & Leveling: 'From above' and 'from below' processes and their effect on register flexibility in diverse communities.
  • Methodological Innovation: Integrating usage-based corpus linguistics with experimental approaches (perceptual and production-based).
